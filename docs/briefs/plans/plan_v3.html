4. App Service vs AKS vs Container Apps — Which to Choose?

Key Decision for Your Team
🚀 Azure App Service P2v3 RECOMMENDED
Why best for this scale:
  • No Kubernetes expertise needed
  • Auto-scale 2 → 10 instances in minutes
  • Built-in CI/CD from Azure DevOps
  • 500 req/sec? 2 instances handle it easily
  • Blue-green deployment built-in (slots)
  • Much cheaper at this traffic level
  • Your .NET + Angular team can deploy today
💰 Cost: ~₹8–12L/month for P2v3 × 3 instances
🐳 AKS Standard IF TEAM KNOWS K8s
Use AKS only if:
  • Team has Kubernetes experience
  • Need fine-grained pod-level control
  • Want KEDA event-driven scaling
  • Multiple microservices with different scale needs
  • Future-proofing for larger events
💰 Cost: ~₹10–15L/month for 3-node cluster
🔄 Azure Container Apps MIDDLE GROUND
Best of both worlds:
  • Serverless containers — no K8s management
  • Scale to zero on idle (save cost)
  • KEDA built-in for event-driven scale
  • Simpler than AKS, more flexible than App Service
  • Good choice if budget is very tight
💰 Cost: Pay-per-use — can be cheaper than App Service
💡
Our Recommendation for Your Team: Use Azure App Service P2v3 for all 7 microservices (one App Service per service). Your .NET team already knows how to deploy to App Service. No Kubernetes learning curve. At 500 req/sec peak, this is the pragmatic, low-risk choice. Switch to AKS only if you outgrow App Service — which at 200M over 45 days, you won't.
